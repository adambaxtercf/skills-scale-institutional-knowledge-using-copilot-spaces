# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered design solutions by designing user flows, wireframes, and prototypes. They ensure solutions meet usability standards and work closely with Product Managers and Developers during planning and review phases.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Ensure solutions meet accessibility and usability standards
- Participate in design reviews and incorporate feedback
- Collaborate on defining acceptance criteria for user-facing features

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve task completion rates
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Participate in sprint planning and design reviews
- Collaborate with Product Managers on requirements and priorities
- Provide design specs and assets to Developers
- Receive and incorporate technical feedback from Developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on requirements, validate design decisions against business goals
- **Developers**: Hand off designs, clarify implementation details, receive technical constraints
- **Project Managers**: Provide effort estimates for design work, flag blockers

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between stakeholders, Product Managers, and Developers by analyzing requirements, modeling processes, and validating business needs. They ensure the team understands and addresses the underlying business problems.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze and document business requirements
- Model processes and workflows
- Facilitate stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Clarify technical requirements for Developers
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure clear, actionable requirements for the team
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Identify and resolve requirement ambiguities early

### Typical Communication
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Document and communicate requirements to Product Managers and Developers
- Review deliverables against original requirements

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Refine scope and prioritization, validate business value
- **Developers**: Clarify technical requirements, answer questions during implementation
- **Stakeholders**: Gather input, validate requirements, communicate progress

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads gather post-release feedback, monitor support channels, and identify recurring issues. They share insights to inform product improvements and participate in release and retrospective meetings.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor support channels and triage customer issues
- Identify recurring issues and escalate critical problems
- Gather and communicate customer feedback to the team
- Participate in release planning and retrospectives
- Document known issues and workarounds

### Goals
- Improve customer satisfaction and reduce support ticket volume
- Ensure critical issues are promptly escalated and addressed
- Provide actionable feedback to inform product improvements

### Typical Communication
- Report trends and incidents to Product Managers and Project Managers
- Escalate critical support issues to relevant team members
- Participate in release meetings and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share customer feedback and feature requests
- **Project Managers**: Report support trends, escalate urgent issues
- **Developers**: Communicate bug reports, clarify reproduction steps

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads advise on risk management, review features for security impact, and lead incident response for security issues. They ensure the team maintains a strong security posture throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Advise on security risks and best practices during planning
- Review features and architecture for security impact
- Lead incident response for security-related issues
- Maintain and update the security incident runbook
- Ensure compliance with security policies and standards

### Goals
- Minimize security vulnerabilities and incidents
- Ensure timely detection and response to security threats
- Foster a security-aware culture within the team

### Typical Communication
- Consult during planning for risk register and security requirements
- Review features and PRs for security considerations before release
- Lead incident management and communication for security escalations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Contribute to risk register, escalate security concerns
- **Developers**: Review code for security issues, provide security guidance
- **Product Managers**: Advise on security implications of feature decisions

## Onboarding Checklist for New Roles

Use this checklist when onboarding a new team member to any of the above roles.

### General Onboarding
- [ ] Review the [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md)
- [ ] Read the [Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) document (this document)
- [ ] Familiarize yourself with the [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) process
- [ ] Understand the [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) workflow
- [ ] Review the [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) guidelines

### Role-Specific Onboarding

#### Developers
- [ ] Set up development environment and access to repositories
- [ ] Review codebase architecture and coding standards
- [ ] Meet with tech lead to understand current sprint priorities

#### Product Managers
- [ ] Review product roadmap and current backlog
- [ ] Meet with stakeholders to understand business priorities
- [ ] Familiarize yourself with success metrics and dashboards

#### Project Managers
- [ ] Review current project status and risk register
- [ ] Meet with Product Managers and Developers to understand ongoing work
- [ ] Set up access to project boards and communication channels

#### UX Designer
- [ ] Review existing design systems and style guides
- [ ] Meet with Product Managers to understand current priorities
- [ ] Set up design tools and access to design repositories

#### Business Analyst
- [ ] Review existing requirements documentation
- [ ] Meet with Product Managers and stakeholders to understand current projects
- [ ] Familiarize yourself with stakeholder communication channels

#### Support Lead
- [ ] Gain access to support channels and ticketing systems
- [ ] Review known issues and workaround documentation
- [ ] Meet with Product Managers to understand current product state

#### Security Lead
- [ ] Review security policies and incident runbooks
- [ ] Gain access to security scanning and monitoring tools
- [ ] Meet with Developers to understand the codebase and architecture
